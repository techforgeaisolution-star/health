What we offer

Three screening services, one goal

Each tool uses a dedicated machine learning model trained on clinical or voice-derived datasets. Pick the assessment that matches the data you have — none of them replace a doctor.

01

Diabetes risk assessment

Ideal when you have lab-style inputs: pregnancies, glucose, blood pressure, skin thickness, insulin, BMI, diabetes pedigree, and age.

  • What you get: a positive/negative style prediction plus a visual risk score and factor breakdown.
  • Best for: learning how metabolic markers relate to model output — educational use only.
  • Output: lifestyle-oriented precautions and an optional PDF report on the results page.
Open diabetes form →
02

Heart disease risk assessment

Uses classic cardiac features: age, sex, chest pain type, resting BP, cholesterol, fasting sugar, ECG, max heart rate, angina, ST depression, slope, vessels, and thalassemia flag.

  • What you get: an instant cardiac-risk style readout with charts for cholesterol, age, heart-rate reserve, and ST change.
  • Best for: exploring how structured heart-disease datasets map to model scores.
  • Output: precaution list and downloadable summary for your records (not for diagnosis).
Open heart disease form →
03

Parkinson's speech screening

Based on voice measurements (MDVP fundamentals, jitter, shimmer, noise ratios, nonlinear metrics, spread, D2, PPE, and related fields).

  • What you get: a risk score aligned with published voice biomarker research, with jitter/shimmer/HNR/PPE highlighted.
  • Best for: demos and coursework around biomedical signal / speech features.
  • Output: guidance-style precautions and PDF export — always confirm with a neurologist.

How it works

From your numbers to a clear report

No account required. Everything runs in your browser session until you submit the form.

  1. Choose a service — diabetes, heart, or Parkinson's — based on which measurements you can provide.
  2. Enter validated ranges — fields highlight green or red as you type so outliers are obvious.
  3. Review the result — risk band, gauge, factor bars, and plain-language precautions; download a PDF if you need to share or archive.
